We're Not Good Enough (2024)

The story unfolds from an accidental encounter, where two individuals, born on the same day of the same month, not only fall in love with the same man but also frequently buy the same clothes. Once rivals in love during their youth, they spend ten years tracking each other's traces on Facebook, fantasizing about each other's well-being, only to unexpectedly reunite.

2.
After Marriage (2024)

This series about love and marriage features one to two narrators per episode, constantly overturning and repositioning the understanding of characters and events through switching narrators, perspectives, and time. This highlights that the world of love has always been like a maze, with multiple versions, leaving those involved puzzled. The series is divided into four parts: divorce, infidelity, mistaken love, and restoration, exploring the situations or doubts each person has experienced: What is love? Why get married? Why cheat? Why divorce? Why seek revenge? How to let go? How to recover? How to stay together until old age? Perhaps, only by daring to confront the ugliness, darkness, and brokenness of human nature can one have a chance to mend a fractured relationship.

3.
Ripley (2024)

Tom Ripley is a con artist barely making ends meet in New York City in the early 1960s. A wealthy man hires him to persuade his wandering son to return home from Italy. Tom accepts the job, taking his first step into the complex life of deception, fraud, and murder. This limited series is adapted from Patricia Highsmith's bestselling novel "Tom Ripley".

The Woman Who Wants to Cook and the Woman Who Wants to Eat (2024)

It has been announced that NHK's drama "Women Who Want to Cook and Women Who Want to Eat" starring Miho Kawai will produce a sequel! 5.
About Us Unknown (2024)

Wei Qian (Qiu Yuchen), orphaned at a young age, shoulders the responsibility of supporting the family, raising his younger sister Wei Lili (Lin Sitin) besides himself, and unexpectedly adopts his younger brother Wei Zhiyuan (Huang Hongxuan). For the sake of his siblings, Wei Qian works tirelessly to earn money, and fortunately, his childhood friend San Pang (Kim Jae-hoon) pulls him back on track. As life gradually improves, Xiao Yuan confesses that he is in love with his brother and even kisses Wei Qian forcefully. Out of anger, Wei Qian sends Xiao Yuan abroad, only to realize that something seems to be missing in his heart. Will the two, who are not related by blood, end up as family, brothers, or will they be able to cross that forbidden line and become lovers?

6.
My Food Guide (2024)

This program sees director Chen Xiaoqing stepping from behind the camera to in front of it, exploring food from his own perspective and delving into the stories behind it. In this show, Chen Xiaoqing travels to eight different regions of China, embarking on a flavorful exploration journey with different guides. Among these guides are Chen Xiaoqing's old friends as well as new acquaintances. They come from diverse backgrounds, including philosophers, archaeologists, anthropologists, musicians, professional chefs, economists, and writers. Chen Xiaoqing brings more people together through food; he believes, "Food is not only delicious, but also a kind of adhesive that enables people to have more understanding and communication."

7.
Thank You for Warming Me Up (2024)

Lin Tuo (Lin Yi), in the prime of his life, meets An Zhijue (Li Landi) at a summer internship recruitment event during their junior year. They fall in love at first sight and after getting to know each other better, they start a relationship. As graduation season approaches, the two embark on their journey from the ivory tower to society, working part-time jobs, attending interviews, and planning for the future together. Although the journey of life does not proceed smoothly, with encouragement from each other, they gradually get back on track. However, just when everything seems to be going smoothly, Lin Tuo is diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, commonly known as ALS. ALS puts an end to Lin Tuo's seemingly ordinary yet hopeful life. Though his life is shortened, Lin Tuo bravely challenges the disease, supported by the warmth and love of his family, lover, friends, and colleagues. Lin Tuo's companions and fellow patients are also inspired by him, reexamining their attitudes toward life, learning to cherish the present moment, and deciding to live each day with no regrets, full of hope.

Radiation Season 1 (2024)

"Radiation" is adapted from one of the greatest video game series of all time, telling the story of rich and poor in a world almost devoid of everything. 200 years after the apocalypse, a peaceful resident from a comfortable radiation shelter is forced to return to the surface, only to be shocked by the wasteland that awaits her.
